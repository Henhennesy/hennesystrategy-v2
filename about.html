About Mike Hennesy

A strategist and product innovator focused on helping organizations translate emerging cannabinoid and psychedelic science into meaningful human outcomes and practical business decisions.

Mike Hennesy is a strategist and product innovator focused on helping organizations translate emerging cannabinoid and psychedelic science into meaningful human outcomes and practical business decisions. His work sits at the intersection of product development, research strategy, regulatory engagement, and commercialization.

Over the past decade, Mike has helped shape the cannabis industry through product innovation, clinical research support, and science-informed strategy. Public profiles describe a career that has included research and development leadership, new product design, educational initiatives, and a broader role in translating scientific and therapeutic ideas into real-world application. He joined Wana in 2014 and helped grow into innovation leadership over the years, bringing together science fluency with operator instincts and commercial practicality.

Mike’s public record also reflects a strong commitment to industry leadership and scientific engagement. He serves on the Governing Board of Colorado’s Institute of Cannabis Research and on the board of Colorado Leads. He has also appeared as a speaker, moderator, and contributor across science, policy, and commercialization forums, including CannMed, Cannabis Science Conference, MJ Unpacked, and other public-facing educational events.

His research involvement includes coauthorship on a peer-reviewed, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled study on the effects of CBN with and without CBD on sleep quality. That work reflects a broader interest in strengthening the evidence base around cannabinoid products and advancing more rigorous conversations about efficacy, safety, and therapeutic potential.

Through Hennesy Strategy, Mike works with companies, academic centers, foundations, and other partners that want more than trend-chasing. He is especially well suited to engagements that require judgment across science, product strategy, evidence generation, regulation, and practical execution.

Approach

How I work

I tend to be most useful when a team is facing ambiguity.

That might be a promising product idea with unclear evidence, a technical story that has not yet been translated into a commercial strategy, a research agenda that needs sharper purpose, or a fast-moving opportunity that needs disciplined pressure-testing. I ask good questions, get to the heart of the issue quickly, and help teams make smarter decisions earlier.

I care about ideas that are both exciting and defensible. The most interesting concept in the room is not always the one worth building. My role is to help distinguish between what is merely compelling and what has real potential to become credible, differentiated, and useful in the world.
